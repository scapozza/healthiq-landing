Kids & Neurodevelopment

Understanding your child's genetic wiring — not labeling it

Certain genetic variants are associated with ADHD-related traits (DRD4, DAT1), dyslexia risk (DCDC2, KIAA0319), and sensory processing tendencies. Knowing these early doesn't define your child — it gives you context for how they learn, process, and thrive.

Kyronix presents pediatric genetic insights with care and nuance — focused on what's actionable for parents and educators, not on stigma or labels. Every child's profile is interpreted with the understanding that genetics is one input, not a destiny.

🧠

ADHD-Associated Variants

DRD4 7-repeat and DAT1 10-repeat alleles are associated with ADHD-related attention and impulsivity traits. Understanding this helps calibrate learning environments.

📖

Reading & Language Variants

DCDC2 and KIAA0319 variants are among the most replicated genetic associations with dyslexia risk. Early identification opens the door to targeted interventions.

🌱

Nutrition for Development

MTHFR status in children affects folate metabolism — relevant for neurodevelopment, especially in the early years and with ADHD.

🏃

Athletic Development

ACTN3 genotype is fixed from birth. Knowing your child's power vs. endurance profile early helps guide sport selection and training emphasis.

☀️

Vitamin D & Bone Health

VDR variants are especially relevant in children — affecting bone mineralization during peak growth years. Track D3 levels and supplement if needed.

🛡️

Immune & Allergy Tendencies

HLA variants and immune gene patterns can indicate allergy or autoimmune predispositions — giving parents context for unusual symptom patterns.
