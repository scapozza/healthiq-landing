Biological Age

Are you aging faster than your calendar?

Chronological age tells you how long you've been alive. Biological age tells you how old your cells actually are. The gap between the two is where longevity interventions live.

Kyronix estimates your biological age from a composite of your lab markers (HbA1c, CRP, lipids, kidney function), wearable data (HRV, resting HR, sleep stages), and your genetic aging predisposition — then tracks it over time so you can see if your interventions are actually working.

APOE Cardiovascular Risk

The gene variant that changes everything about your heart health plan

APOE determines how your body processes fats and cholesterol — and it's one of the strongest genetic risk factors for both cardiovascular disease and Alzheimer's. Your variant (ε2, ε3, or ε4) should shape your dietary approach, lipid monitoring frequency, and lifestyle strategy.

Kyronix cross-references your APOE status with your actual lipid panel results and cardiovascular metrics from Apple Health to give you a personalized risk picture — and what to do about it.

Sleep & Epigenetics

Sleep is the most powerful longevity lever you control

DNA methylation — the epigenetic process underlying biological aging — is profoundly influenced by sleep quality. Poor sleep accelerates methylation drift; optimal sleep can slow it.

Your CLOCK and PER3 variants determine your natural chronotype. Kyronix uses this to recommend an optimal sleep window for your biology — not the societal default of 10pm to 6am.

Combine your genetic chronotype with Apple Health sleep stage data and Kyronix surfaces the precise adjustments that move the needle on your biological age.
